Margin Funding

How It Works

Margin funding allows investors to borrow funds to purchase additional securities, leveraging their investment capital. By using margin accounts, you can buy more shares than you could with your own funds alone.

Benefits for Investors

  • This can amplify potential returns, enabling you to capitalize on market opportunities more quickly.
  • It also offers increased flexibility in managing your portfolio.

Risks Associated

  • While margin trading can enhance gains, it also increases risk.
  • If the value of your investments declines, you may face margin calls, requiring you to deposit more funds or sell assets to maintain your position.

Securities Lending and Borrowing

How It Works

This service allows investors to lend their securities to other investors or institutions for a fee, while borrowers can access stocks for short selling or other strategies.

Benefits for Investors

  • Lenders can earn additional income from their investments without selling them.
  • Borrowers can enhance their trading strategies by gaining access to shares they do not own.

Risks Associated

  • Lenders face the risk of borrower default, although collateral is typically required.
  • Borrowers must be mindful of potential losses if the stock price rises unexpectedly.

Overview

Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs)

Overview

Trade ETFs that track various indices, sectors, or asset classes, offering a flexible investment option similar to stocks.

Benefits

  • ETFs provide diversification, liquidity, and lower expense ratios compared to mutual funds.

Risks

  • Like stocks, ETFs are subject to market volatility and trading costs.
